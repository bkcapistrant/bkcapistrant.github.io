About Me

Hello! I am a second year Astronomy Ph.D. student at the University of Florida, studying under Dr. Jason Dittmann on the subjects of stars and exoplanets. I enjoy working with data from the Transiting Exoplanet Survey Satellite (TESS) mission with the goal of furthering our understanding of exoplanet-star interactions.

I graduated from the University of Wisconsin-Madison with dual B.S. degrees in Physics and Astronomy-Physics from the School of Letters and Science, and a certificate (minor) in Mathematics. At UW I researched under NASA Hubble Postdoctoral Fellow Melinda Soares-Furtado, Prof. Ke Zhang, Dr. Adam Shaefer and Prof. Christy Tremonti, and Prof. Andrew Vanderburg at MIT. I worked on projects on a variety of topics ranging from locating density-bound HII regions in star-forming galaxies, CO abundance in protoplanetary disks, to eventually the field of stars and exoplanets, which I continue to research now.

Check out my Research page for more information on some of those and my current projects!

Hobbies

Outside of astronomy I enjoy traveling and landscape photography (some examples of my photography can be seen below). I also enjoy music and play the drums, guitar, piano, and just about any instrument I can get my hands on.

While researching, I've developed a great interest in scientific visualization. I've created animations and renders like the ones on my research page with Blender and Photoshop. Recently I have been working with AI art and design to create visualizations for talks in an effort to generate interest in my work and the field of astronomy in general. All of the visualizations on this website were created by me.

You can find more of my photography on my photography website that includes my entire portfolio.
